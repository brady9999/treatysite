Histroy of Treaty 1

Thousands Of People Gathered In Lower Fort Garry

people gathered for the treaty 1 negotiations that happend in Lower Fort Garry (just north of Winnipeg) back in August, 1871. the negotiations took around nine days to compleate.

Fun Fact: there was a newspaper from america that came to witness the negotiations


Historic Promises

Each man, woman and child is to be given a gratuity. or a one time payment of $3, and $15 per family of five yearly. the government agreed to build and maintain a school on each reserve. and to prohibit the sale of liquor on reserves each band was to receive a large amount of land, 160 acres to be exact.

Fun Fact: $15 dollars in 1871 is worth $306.12 today (CAD)


Why Is Treaty 1 Importent

treaty 1 helped establish western canada. there was a delcoration that says the treaty will be in place for "as long as the sun shines, the grass grows and the river flows.


How Treaties Affect Us Today

they provide a way for people to live together and sharing land that was origionaly occupied by the first nations people. they help operate poeration, partnership and reconciliation. they also help spread diffrent cultures around manitoba and canada


Urban Reserves

What are urban reserves

urban reserves are reserves within a urban area (like a city). urban reserves are all over Manitoba, like Portage La Prairie, Polo park and now on kenaston. most reserves are in rural areas where they are far away from big citys like Winnipeg where there are hundreds of job oppertunitys. with urban reserves it gives first nations people who live on reserves more of a chance to have jobs and still live on traditional native-land.
