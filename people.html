Peoples of Treaty 1

Brokenhead (Ojibway)

Brokenhead first nation is 40 miles north of Winnipeg or 25 miles northeast of Selkirk. Brokenhead became a community in manitoba as of May 2010. the first nation has a registared population of 1,733 people.

Long Plain (Ojibway and Dakota)

Long Plain was one of the first nation communities to sign treaty 1 in 1871. Long Plain has over 4500 people and has 3 reserves and 2 of them are urban reserves, the urban reserves are in Winnipeg and Portage Le Prairie.

Peguis (Ojibway and Cree)

Peguis first nations has a population of over 10k of Cree and Objiway descents Peguis is located 190kn north of Winnipeg. the communitie was named after Cheif Peguis who lead the Saultaux people from Ontario to Manitoba iin the late 1700's

Roseau River (Anishiabe)

Roseau River has a population of 2000. Roseau River signed Treaty 1 in late 1871. finally got there land claim in 2011, with asettlement that is held in trust for future generations. Roseau River is approximately 1 hour south of Winnipeg.

Sagkeeng (Anicinabe)

Sagkeeng was one of the first nation communites that signed Treaty 1. the communitie has a approximate 21,600 acres of land as of 1874 currently has a population of 3,352.

Sandy Bay (Ojibway)

Sandy Bay first nation has a mission to deliver culturally appropriate support services through accoutability, respect, transparent communication and leadership or the better of the community.

Swan lake (Anishinabe)

Swan lake First Nation (SLFN) is in south central Manitoba. Swan Lake is devided into 4 areas, SLFM #7 is the main communitie, #7a Carberry which is residential and commercial development, #8 Indian Gardens, 3/4 of the land is under agricultural lease and #8a Headingley, mainly consists of commercial development.
